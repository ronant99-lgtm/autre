Area to avoid — Do not shop or get gas at the Poplar & Highland intersection. Head further east on Poplar (past Highland) or use stores on Union Avenue downtown. Miss Cordelia's on Mud Island is the best option near the center.

🗺 Activities & Places to See

Must-sees
🎖 Must-see

National Civil Rights Museum

Located in the historic Lorraine Motel, site of MLK's assassination. Deeply moving and immersive. Plan 3–4 hours. Free parking nearby. Closed on Tuesdays.

A must visit while you are here. Incredibly disturbing look at history but the museum is done so well.

🌳 Outdoors

Shelby Farms Park

4,500 acres of green space in East Memphis (~20 min from downtown). Trails, lake, playground, restaurants inside the park. Perfect for a family weekend outing.

A beautiful park — tons of trails, walking paths, restaurants inside the park. A great place to get outside on the weekend.
Website 📍 Map ★ 4.8 · 12,000 reviews
🌸 Nature

Memphis Botanic Garden

Beautiful botanical gardens. Currently featuring a giant TROLL sculpture exhibit — great with kids. Open daily 9am–6pm.

It's beautiful — they have a new exhibit with giant TROLLS!
Website 📍 Map ★ 4.7 · 3,000 reviews
🦁 Zoo

Memphis Zoo

One of the best zoos in the country. 3,500+ animals, splash pad, sea lion shows. Open 9am–5pm (6pm on Saturdays).

It's a great zoo! Must see.

🛒 Grocery Shopping

Practical tips from Valerie
✅ Recommended

Miss Cordelia's · Mud Island

Quality neighborhood grocery on Mud Island — ideal if staying downtown. Deli, fresh produce, great beer selection. Open 7am–9pm (8am weekends).

Website 📍 Map ★ 4.6
✅ Safe zones

Kroger · Whole Foods · Fresh Market

Stick to stores east of Highland on Poplar, or those on Union Avenue downtown. ⚠ Avoid the Kroger at Poplar & Highland.

Poplar Avenue is the main road — once you cross the Highland intersection, you don't have to be as careful. Same for getting gas — go a bit further out or on Union.
